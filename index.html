Biography

Sulman is an award-winning agricultural and applied economist dedicated to implementing innovative research techniques to inform sustainable policies for rural development and the agri-food sector. In 2023, he was recognised as the Best Collaborative Masters in Agricultural and Applied Economics (CMAAE) student, an accolade awarded within the CMAAE programme that included eight major universities across Eastern and Southern Africa. Earlier that year, he was also acknowledged as the second-best master’s student in the Faculty of Agriculture at the University of Nairobi. Sulman is currently a Research Fellow at the International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA) and has undertaken numerous consultancy engagements with international research organisations. His research interests span production and ecological economics, causal analysis, microeconomics, Bayesian econometrics, statistics, and quantitative modelling.
